States, on the issue dated July 24, 2021, earning 15.9&nbsp;million streams, 1.1&nbsp;million radio airplay impressions, and selling 140,000 copies in its first week of release. The song replaced "Butter" from the top of the chart, marking the first time an artist had self-replaced themselves since [[Drake (musician)|Drake]]\'s "[[In My Feelings]]" replaced his own "[[Nice for What]]" in 2018. The song also became BTS\' fifth number-one hit on the chart in ten months and two weeks, making them the artist with the fastest accumulation of five number-one singles since [[Michael Jackson]] did so in 1988. Additionally, it became their fourth single to debut atop the Hot 100, extending their record as the group with the most number-one single debuts (tied with [[Justin Bieber]] and Drake, but behind [[Ariana Grande]], who has five). On the [[Digital Songs]] chart, "Permission to Dance" became BTS\' eighth number-one, extending their record as the group with the most number-one hits on the chart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9603058/bts-permission-to-dance-number-one-hot-100/|title=BTS Blasts Onto Hot 100 at No. 1 With \'Permission to Dance,\' The Kid LAROI & Justin Bieber Bow at No. 3 With \'Stay\'|last=Trust|first=Gary|date=July 19, 2021|website=Billboard|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210719172450/https://www.billboard.com/articles/news/9603058/bts-permission-to-dance-number-one-hot-100/|archive-date=July 19, 2021|url-status=live|access-date=July 22, 2021}}</ref> The song debuted atop the [[Billboard charts#Other charts|\'\'Billboard\'\' Hot Trending Songs]], a chart powered by [[Twitter]] which tracks the most-discussed songs on the platform.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9649598/bts-permission-to-dance-hot-trending-songs-charts/|title=BTS\' \'Permission to Dance\' Leads Inaugural Hot Trending Songs Charts, Powered by Twitter|author=<!--Not stated; staff-->|date=October 22, 2021|website=Billboard|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211022225239/https://www.billboard.com/articles/business/chart-beat/9649598/bts-permission-to-dance-hot-trending-songs-charts/|archive-date=October 22, 2021|url-status=live|access-date=October 23, 2021}}</ref>\n\nIn September, Oricon reported that the single had crossed 100&nbsp;million streams in Japan, becoming BTS\' fifth song to do so.
